Basic Action Annotations

b1 Must be near an upper turnbuckle.
b2 Must be standing on the top turnbuckle.
b3 Press the necessary button while walking toward the left, right, or bottom ropes.
b4 Must be near your tag partner.
b5 Must be in a Triple Tag Team match.
b6 Must be near chair. If you drop the chair on the floor, it cannot be picked up again
b7 Must be holding chair. Chair can only be used once.

Standing Annotations

i1 Must be done outside ring, while the ref is down, or in no DQ matches.
i2 Stuns opponents if their health is low.
i3 Can be used to throw opponents over the top rope if you're standing close to the ropes.
i4 Eye Gouge / Choke executes at random on 3 Button Sega Genesis Controllers.

Grapple Annotations

g1 Can be used to throw opponents over the top rope if you're standing close to the ropes.
g2 Stuns opponents if their health is low.
g3 Must be pinning opponent.

Grapple Annotations

d1 Must be on the top rope.
d2 Can jump from top rope to outside of ring.
d3 Executes finisher if the opponent's health is low.
d4 Atomic Drop / Back Breaker executes at random on 3 Button Sega Genesis Controllers.

Win Conditions

Different match types will have different win conditions.

Pinfall: Cover your opponent while the ref counts to 3.

Knockout: Drain your opponents health bar.

Count Out: Keep your opponent outside of the ring while the ref counts to 10

Elimination: Throw your opponent out of the ring.

One-on-One

One Fall: A standard 1 on 1 match. Win via pinfall or count out.

Brawl: A no disqualification match. Win via knockout.

Tournament: A 1-on-1, Eleven round tournament for the WWF Championship.

Tag Team

One Fall: A standard tag match. Win via pinfall or count out.

Brawl: A no disqualification tag match. Win via knockout.

Tournament: A tag team, 5 round tournament for the WWF Tag Team Championship.

Triple Tag Team

One Fall: A standard 3-on-3 tag match. Win via pinfall or count out.

Brawl: A no disqualification 3-on-3 tag match. Win via knockout.

Royal Rumble

A 12 man over-the-top Battle Royal. The match starts with 2 participants. A new participant enters until the ring has 6 total participants. A new participant enters each time a wrestler is eliminated until all only one participant remains in the ring.

Striking Logic

  • Deliver 3 consecutive strikes to knock your opponent down (4 kicks on Sega Genesis).
  • If your opponent strikes back and inerrupts your striking, deliver 2 consecutive strikes to knock your opponent down.
  • If your opponent's health is in the red, the last strike will daze your opponent instead of knocking them down.
  • If your opponent's health is empty, 1 strike will knock them down.

Combinations

There is no official combination system in WWF Royal Rumble. However, you can chain moves together based on distance and positioning.

Example: Atomic Drop into a Running Drop Kick

Example: Body Splash into a Running Elbow Drop

Top Rope Shenanigans

If your opponent is on the top rope, you can knock them off by runnign at the turnbuckle. If you're playing against the CPU, they will automatically position themselves for this counter. Jumping at the right time will result in the CPU opponent crashing into the turnbuckle before executing the Elbow Drop.

Tag Team Strategy

Cut off the Ring: When your opponent is in trouble, they will immediately go to their corner to tag their partner. Standing between your opponent and his partner will allow you to cut them off before they can make the tag.

Interference: The non-legal participant will interfere in the following scenarios:

  • During a pinfall, the partner of the participant being pinned will attempt to break up the pin unless the participant's health is completely empty.
  • The non-legal participant will get involved if the fight ends up on the outside of the ring
  • If you stand too close to the non-legal participant while they are on the apron, they will execute a sleeper hold capable of initiating a stunned status.

Royal Rumble Strategy

There are 4 different ways to eliminate an opponent from the Royal Rumble

Rebound Hip Toss: This is the fastest and easiest way to eliminate an opponent as the opponent's health is not considered. Irish whip your opponent and get close to the ropes to execute the hip toss and eliminate your opponent. With this in mind, it's important to use caution when running toward your opponent as you may run into a hip toss.

Knock Opponent off of Top Rope: You can knock your opponent off of the top rope to the outside, though this will have to be timed perfectly. Use caution when climbing the turnbuckle in a Rumble match to avoid elimination.

Standing Hip Toss / Atomic Drop: When an opponent is worn down enough for elimination, the grapple indicator will begin flashing. If your back is to the nearest rope, execute a Hip Toss to eliminate your opponent. If your opponent's back is facing the rope and you are close, execute an Atomic Drop to eliminate your opponent. If your health is low, it's wise to stay close to the center of the ring and avoid losing grapples when near the ropes.
